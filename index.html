The AI Commons

A living, cross-campus collection of AI-generated tools, governance frameworks, teaching resources, departmental policies, and interdisciplinary projects from every college, department, and office at Lewis University.

When a nursing simulation inspires a physics visualization—when an aviation safety decoder shapes a criminal justice case analyzer—when a university AI policy sparks departmental dialogue in six colleges simultaneously—that’s the Commons at work. Not a repository. A culture.

Where Disciplines Converge

The AI Commons isn’t organized by silos—it’s organized by resonance. A computer science student’s prompt library lives alongside a theology professor’s ethical framework. The interdisciplinary resources here exist in multiple homes at once, because that’s how knowledge actually works.

Lateral Inspiration

A biology professor’s AI-generated lab safety protocol becomes the template for a chemistry department’s hazard system. Neither planned it. The Commons made it visible.

Policy Cascade

The university’s AI Use Policy ripples outward—each college adapts it, each department interprets it. The Commons captures the entire cascade.

Cross-Pollination

An interdisciplinary AI Ethics course draws case studies from nursing, business, aviation, and criminal justice—all living in the Commons.

Governance Transparency

When every AI policy lives in one discoverable place, governance stops being a black box. Faculty can see the full landscape.

Student Benefit

Students in any program can see how AI is used responsibly across the entire university. A living syllabus of institutional AI literacy.

Institutional Memory

Faculty leave. Grants end. The Commons preserves the intellectual labor so nothing disappears into someone’s hard drive.
