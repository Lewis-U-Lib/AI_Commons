What is the AI Commons?

The AI Commons is Lewis University Library's curated collection of resources created with generative AI. It includes interactive applications, lesson plans, prompt libraries, assessment rubrics, policy frameworks, production workflows, and anything else that demonstrates thoughtful human–AI collaboration in academic contexts. Every contribution is reviewed, cataloged, and made openly available.

Who can contribute?

Any member of the Lewis University community — faculty, librarians, and staff. If you've built something with AI that supports teaching, learning, research, or university operations, it belongs here. We provide two paths: a simple submission form for non-technical contributors, and a GitHub-based workflow for those comfortable with code.

Why does this exist?

AI-assisted creation is happening across every discipline at Lewis. The AI Commons makes that work visible, discoverable, and reusable — so a nursing simulation built in one course can inspire a chemistry visualization in another. It's institutional knowledge-sharing in the Lasallian tradition: together and by association.
